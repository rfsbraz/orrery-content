# Licensing

## The content is public domain

Everything under `content/` is released under [CC0 1.0 Universal](LICENSE): a
full waiver of copyright and related rights, **including the EU database right**
(Directive 96/9/EC), which is the one that would otherwise restrict reuse of a
compiled dataset like this one.

In plain terms: take it, fork it, sell it, build on it. No permission needed, no
attribution required, no conditions. Facts about books are not ours to own, and
the point of assembling them in the open is that the work only has to be done
once.

Attribution is welcome and never required.

## Why CC0 and not a share-alike licence

Share-alike would protect the commons from enclosure, which is a real concern,
at the cost of making the data incompatible with most projects that might want
it. For a factual reference dataset the incompatibility is the bigger loss.
Wikidata made the same call for the same reason.

## What the waiver does not cover

CC0 can only waive rights we actually hold. It does not reach:

- **Author portraits and cover images.** These are third-party works, each
  carrying its own licence and credit in the content files (`portraitCredit`,
  `coverCredit`). Check the credit before reusing an image.
- **Human-contributed art.** It carries its artist's name and the terms it was
  given under, and those terms govern rather than this file.
- **Generated illustrations.** They carry `sketchCredit: Generated for Orrery
  (gpt-image-1)`. We waive whatever rights we hold in them, but the copyright
  status of model output is unsettled in several jurisdictions and we cannot
  grant more than we have.
- **Quotations** from authors, interviews and press inside descriptions. Those
  are quoted as quotation and remain their rights-holders'.

## The tooling

The scripts and agent skills (`scripts/`, `.claude/`) are tooling rather than
content and sit outside the CC0 waiver. They are undeclared for now. If you want
to reuse them, open an issue and ask.
