## Events added

- **`turn-coat-number-one-2009`** (franchise, med). Turn Coat (2009) debuts
  at #1 on the NYT hardcover list - first of an unbroken run of #1 debuts
  through Skin Game (2014), the moment the series stops building a
  readership and becomes a guaranteed bestseller. Sources: Wikipedia's Turn
  Coat article (citing NYT "Best Sellers", April 24 2009) and Newsweek 2020,
  independently confirming "the five most recent books have all debuted as
  #1 New York Times bestsellers."
- **`cinder-spires-deal-2013`** (franchise, med). The 2012 Reddit AMA reveal
  and 2013 seven-figure, three-book Roc deal for Cinder Spires - Butcher's
  first new ongoing series since Codex Alera, the publisher betting on an
  unproven genre against his name alone. Sources: jim-butcher.com's repost
  of the Publishers Weekly deal announcement, Reactor's account of the AMA
  (Butcher's own "League of Extraordinary Gentlemen meets Sherlock meets
  Hornblower" quote), and Karen Woodward's blog quoting Butcher's original
  August 2012 status update.

## The 2014-2020 gap: ruled out, with evidence

The scaffold flagged "reported personal hardship" as the real cause behind
Skin Game to Peace Talks, deliberately uncarried. I found the specific
claim: a New York Times profile (June 2025, 25th anniversary of the series)
has Butcher discuss depression and a suicide attempt around the Changes
(2010) deadline, in his own words, framed as the first time he has spoken
about it publicly. That held under a search built to break it - every other
outlet carrying the story (a genre newsletter, a partisan Substack, a
fan-forum paywall-bypass copy) cites that one interview, none independent.

Ruling: does not ship. CURATION.md section 6 wants two independent sources
on a living person's health; one profile, however credible and direct, is
one source. Corrected the events.yaml comment to say this precisely (a
single primary profile, not an "aggregator summary" as the prior note put
it) so the next pass doesn't re-search expecting a thinner source than
exists. A second, independent account would clear the bar.

One near-miss: jim-butcher.com's 2015 "Dresden Novels on Hiatus" post reads
like the real explanation at a glance. It's an April Fools joke, self-marked.
Caught before citing; recorded so it isn't mistaken for real next time.
